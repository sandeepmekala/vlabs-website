Procedure


  1. Hang a spring balance on an iron stand using a clamp.

  2. Take an overflow can and fill it with water to the brim of the outlet and place beaker below the overflow outlet of the can to collect the displaced water.

  3. Take one of the solid blocks and weigh it by hanging it on the hook of the spring balance using a thread. Find the weight of the solid in air and note it.

  4. Now, start lowering the block, still attached to the spring balance into the water of the overflow can.

  5. Note the loss of weight of the block as it gets completely immersed in the water.

  6. After the liquid overflows. Note down the new weight in the beaker.



Observations:

  • Weight of block in air = ___ g wt.

  • Weight of the block after being immersed in liquid = ___ g wt.

  • New mass displayed in the balance = ___ g.

  • Loss of weight of block in air = ___ g wt.

  • Mass of water displaced (m) = ___ g.

  • Weight of solution displaced = m x g = ___ g wt.
